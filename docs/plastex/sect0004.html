3 Almost periodic elements

Let $\mathcal A$ be a Banach algebra, and turn $\mathcal A’$ into an $\mathcal A$-bimodule in the usual way,

\[ \langle {a\cdot \mu },{b}\rangle = \langle {\mu },{ba}\rangle , \quad \pi {\mu \cdot a}{b} = \langle {\mu },{ab}\rangle \qquad (a,b\in \mathcal A,\mu \in \mathcal A’). \]

For $\mu \in \mathcal A’$, let $\mathcal R_\mu :\mathcal A\rightarrow \mathcal A’$ be the map $\mathcal R_\mu (a) = a\cdot \mu $. When $\mathcal R_\mu $ is a compact operator, we say that $\mu $ is almost periodic, written $\mu \in {\operatorname {AP}}(\mathcal A)$. We can show that ${\operatorname {AP}}(\mathcal A)$ is a closed $\mathcal A$-submodule of $\mathcal A’$.

We similarly turn $\mathcal A”$ into an $\mathcal A$-bimodule. We define an action of $\mathcal A”$ on $\mathcal A’$ by

\[ \langle {\Phi \cdot \mu },{a}\rangle = \langle {\Phi },{\mu \cdot a}\rangle , \quad \langle {\mu \cdot \Phi },{a}\rangle = \langle {\Phi },{a\cdot \mu }\rangle \qquad (a\in \mathcal A, \mu \in \mathcal A’, \Phi \in \mathcal A”). \]

Then we define two bilinear maps $\square ,\Diamond $ on $\mathcal A”$ by

\[ \langle {\Phi \square \Psi },{\mu }\rangle = \langle {\Phi },{\Psi \cdot \mu }\rangle , \quad \langle {\Phi \Diamond \Psi },{\mu }\rangle = \langle {\Psi },{\mu \cdot \Phi }\rangle \qquad (\Phi ,\Psi \in \mathcal A”, \mu \in \mathcal A’). \]

Then $\square $ and $\Diamond $ are algebra products, called the Arens products. They both extend the product on $\mathcal A$ in the sense that, for $a\in \mathcal A$ and $\Phi \in \mathcal A”$, $a\cdot \Phi = \kappa _{mc A}(a)\square \Phi = \kappa _{\mathcal A}(a)\Diamond \Phi $, and similarly on the right. See [ 4 , ??? ] or [ for further details.

The following result essentially appears a number of times in the literture, but for our purposes, is well treated by Lau in [ 14 , Theorem 5.8 ] .

Proposition 3.1. Let $\mathcal A$ be a Banach algebra, and let $X\subseteq \mathcal A’$ be a closed $\mathcal A$-submodule. Then the following are equivalent:

  1. $X\subseteq {\operatorname {AP}}(\mathcal A)$;

  2. Treating $X’$ as a quotient of $A”$, both Arens products drop to well-defined algebra products on $X’$, these products agree, and the resuling product is jointly weak$^*$-continuous on bounded subsets.

Let $G$ be a locally compact group. We say that $f\in C^ b(G)$ is almost periodic if the set $\{ f_ s : s\in G \} $ is relatively compact in $C^ b(G)$, where $f_ s(t) = f(ts)$ for $t\in G$. Using bounded approximate identities, it is easy (compare with [ 28 ] ) to show that ${\operatorname {AP}}(L^1(G)) = {\operatorname {AP}}(G)$. We know that ${\operatorname {AP}}(G)$ is a closed $*$-subalgebra of $C^ b(G)$, with spectrum $G^{\operatorname {AP}}$ say. Then $G$ is dense in $G^{\operatorname {AP}}$, and extending the product from $G$ to $G^{\operatorname {AP}}$ turns $G^{\operatorname {AP}}$ into a compact group. Furthermore, $G^{\operatorname {AP}}$ then agrees with the Bohr compactification of $G$. See [ 2 ] for further details.

As $A(G)$ can be thought of as being the “dual” to $L^1(G)$ (hence extending the abelian case, when $A(G) = L^1(\hat G)$), it is not surprising that ${\operatorname {AP}}(A(G))$ has been studied. It is customary to write ${\operatorname {AP}}(\hat G)$ for ${\operatorname {AP}}(A(G))$. See the references [ 5 , Section 7 ] , [ 9 ] , [ 14 ] , [ 10 ] , [ 18 ] , and especially [ 3 ] for further details. It would be fair to say that, for a general locally compact group $G$, the above proposition is about all that is known about ${\operatorname {AP}}(\hat G)$ (though see below for our discussion of invariant states).

When $G$ is abelian, by applying Fourier transforms, we can show that ${\operatorname {AP}}(\hat G) = C^*_\delta (G) = \mathfrak b C^*_ r(G)$. Similarly, when $G$ is discrete and amenable, we have ${\operatorname {AP}}(\hat G) = \mathfrak b C^*_ r(G)$ (this follows from inspecting [ 9 , Proposition 2 ] ). We do not know, in general, if ${\operatorname {AP}}(\hat G)$ need be a C$^*$-algebra. Chou defines a group $G$ to have the dual Bohr approximation property if ${\operatorname {AP}}(\hat G) = C^*_\delta (G) = \mathfrak bC^*_ r(G)$. In [ 3 ] , and in Rinder’s correction [ 22 ] , it is shown that there exist compact groups $G$ which fail to have the dual Bohr approximation property. Indeed, the idea is as follows. Let $E\in VN(G)$ be the rank-one operator defined by

\[ E(f) = \Big(\int _ G f \Big) 1_ G \qquad (f\in L^2(G)), \]

where $1_ G$ is the constant function on $G$. Then it is possible for both $\mathcal R_ E$ to be compact, that is, $E\in {\operatorname {AP}}(\hat G)$, and for us to have that $E\not\in C^*_\delta (G)$.
